Who is Mike Grier?

Mike Grier is an American retired ice hockey player. He has played for different teams in the National Hockey League (NHL).

He is best known as the assistant coach at Saint Sebastian’s School.

Mike Grier: Birth Facts, Family, and Childhood

Mike was born on 5 January 1975, in Detroit, Michigan. He is of North American ethnicity and has an American nationality.

He is the son of Bobby Grier. His birth name is Michael James Grier.

His father was the Associate Director of Pro Personnel for the Houston Texans of the National Football League (NFL).

Mike Grier: Education History

Grier studied at St. Sebastian’s School where he started playing ice hockey.

After graduating high school, he attended Boston University.

At the age of 9, he appeared in Sports Illustrated’s “Faces In The Crowd” section in issue 06-18-84/Vol. 60.

Mike Grier: Professional Life and Career

Mike Grier started playing ice hockey in 1996 and he retired in 2011.

He started playing ice hockey professionally after being drafted by the  St. Louis Blues in the ninth round of the 1993 NHL Entry Draft.

He was traded to Washington Capitals to free up roster space on 2 October 2002.

On 4 March 2004, he was traded to the Buffalo Sabres.

He recorded four game-winning goals for the Sabres and contributed to their run to the 2006 Eastern Conference Finals.

He signed with the San Jose Sharks but soon he returned to the Buffalo Sabres on 10 August 2009.

After being a free agent, the Buffalo Sabres did not sign him and he officially announced his retirement from the NHL on December 1, 2011.

He has been a part of the top penalty-killing units for the teams that he played for.
