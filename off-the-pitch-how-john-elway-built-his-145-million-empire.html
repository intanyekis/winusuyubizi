John Elway is a renowned American professional football executive with a decorated 16-year stint as a quarterback for the 'Denver Broncos'. Elway is widely regarded as one of the best quarterbacks to ever step foot on the football field and is statistically deemed the second most prolific passer in NFL history. John is also one of only two players to secure a rushing touchdown in four different Super Bowls and the only quarterback to accomplish this incredible feat.

Elway has made headlines across the globe for a variety of reasons. The revered football sensation has garnered several accolades and one-off records on the NFL stage and has successfully capitalized on his enormous brand value to establish himself as a successful business professional after he retired from the sport. Unsurprisingly, John Elway is one of the wealthiest sports professionals in the world with a mind-boggling $145 million personal net worth.

A Noticeable Flair For The Sport 

His father heavily influenced John Elway's admiration for football. A young and passionate John would watch his father flourish as a focused football coach for the University of Montana and even Washington State. The positivity and support surrounding John would inevitably influence his career path towards making an impact on the sport professionally.

John was a natural on the field and would come into his own as a feared high school quarterback. The emerging athlete was a joy to witness and would often steal the spotlight with his multifaceted ability to run and pass with uniform ferocity.

The budding football professional was referred to as a 'Dual-Threat' on his high school turf, with his bewildering abilities as an athlete proving to be an enormous confidence-booster for his future and professional existence.

With a barrage of scholarship offers to come his way, John would make his way to Stanford University. The athletic stud would significantly impact the football scenario and become the university's star quarterback.

John's time at the University of Stanford would prove immensely fruitful for his career, unsurprisingly drawing the attention of the holy grail of football leagues, the NFL.

A Bumpy Start To An Illustrious Career

John Elway's start into the professional league was a tad shaky. The up-and-comer of the sport would be picked by the 'Baltimore Colts' in the 1983 draft, a decision which did not sit well with Elway.

John Elway was undeniably the 'next big thing' in the sport of football, and his preferred choice of team was duly taken into consideration. Eventually, his wish to play for the 'Denver Broncos' was granted.

The emerging athlete would initially struggle to find his stride as a quarterback. However, luck would be on Elway's side, as renowned quarterback Steve Deberg would suffer a shoulder injury, rendering him incapable of playing.

Elway was quick to take his spot, with the swap acting as a pillar of support in taking his team to the prestigious Super Bowl game in 1986. Elway's team would return to the Super Bowl in 1986 and even 1989 but would fail to garner a victory in either season.

John's credentials as a quarterback would come under the scanner, with several critics doubting the emerging athlete's talent and ability to make it big in the sport.

Becoming A Football Sensation

While Elway's start in the world of professional football was far from glamorous, the budding athlete's steady grasp of confidence and experience through the years would alter his career trajectory for the better.

1997 would prove to be a breakout year for John and the 'Denver Broncos', contributing as a quarterback leading his team to the Super Bowl. While his performance at the prestigious event was nothing spectacular, Elway's team had finally secured their first Super Bowl victory.

This victory would prove to be immensely beneficial for John's self-confidence and stature in the sport. The following year would detail Elway's career-best performance and a Super Bowl victory, with John named the 'MVP' of what would be his final game in the league.

Becoming A Recognized Entrepreneur

John's stint as a football sensation was only the tip of the iceberg of his illustrious career moving forward. After his charismatic exit from the sport, John would capitalize on his brand image and go on to establish five car dealerships under the 'John Elway Autos' banner.

This drastic career change would prove immensely fruitful, with his profitable dealerships sold to American automotive retailer 'AutoNation' for a whopping $82.5 million in 1997.

AutoNation would also capitalize on the 'John Elway' brand name as a marketing strategy for maximum profitability, with the expiration of the deal in 2006 allowing Elway to re-enter the auto dealership industry under his own name once again.

In the years to come, John Elway would inaugurate a Chrysler Jeep dealership in Colorado, two Toyota Scion dealerships in California, and even a Cadillac franchise under the 'Sonic' automotive brand, with his efforts as a self-made businessman proving to be immensely beneficial to his mind-boggling $145 million net worth.

John Elway is undeniably one of the most accomplished sports personalities and entrepreneurs globally. The football sensation has amassed an enormous fan following as an athlete as well as a business professional, with his illustrious career serving as an inspirational tale of passion, commitment, and hard work. Elway has witnessed a dark patch in his career as a football professional and a businessman.

The multi-talented football executive has had to work overtime to extract the best of his abilities as a quarterback on the football field, while a few bad investments in the commercial sector have also influenced his growth as an entrepreneur. Nonetheless, John Elway is at a high point in his professional existence, and one can expect his personal net worth and influence in the sports sector to snowball further shortly.
