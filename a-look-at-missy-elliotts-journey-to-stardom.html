A Look At Missy Elliott's Journey To Stardom

July 2024 · 4 minute read

Quick Links

  • She Dared To Dream
  • Empowering Through Music
  • Paving The Way For Future Female Rappers

Born in Portsmouth, Virginia, Missy Elliott had a musical childhood since she was a part of the church choir in her hometown. After relocating and graduating high school, she created an all-female group to make music. Elliott ventured into a solo career at 25 when she was signed onto a record label to release her albums. While her debut album was a massive success, the sophomore became a bigger hit leading to multiple accolades and critical acclaim. Through her music and lyrics about gender equality and representation, the rapper created her sound, which audiences fell in love with everywhere.

Missy Elliott has amassed a massive $50 million fortune over the years through her contribution to music, sold-out concerts, and brand endorsements. Her songs have become timeless as the new generation of listeners gravitates toward old-school rap. A career that got its beginnings in a choir let’s take a look at the success story of Missy Elliott.

She Dared To Dream

Melissa Arnette Elliott, better known as Missy Elliott, was born in Virginia and was the only child of Ronnie and Patricia Elliot. While her father actively worked in the Marine, Elliott moved a lot with her family from North Carolina to Florida. Due to the father’s abusive behavior, she relocated to Portsmouth, Virginia, at fourteen years of age for school. According to Britannica, Elliot found interest in rapping from a young age and formed her group Sista in 1991. The female R&B group created demo tracks for submissions to various record labels to get a big break.

Simultaneously, she became friends with Timothy Mosley, who had been interested in DJing since he was a child. He took on the stage name of Timbaland and began creating beats for Sista. They collaborated on tracks together, and their produced track caught the interest of Elektra Records’ executive DeVante Swing who signed Elliott’s band to his record label. Along with producing music for other artists, Elliott’s Sista and Timbaland co-produced the LP 4 All the Sistas Around da World, as noted by Cheat Sheet. They became a songwriting and producing team who collaborated with artists like 702 and Aaliyah. While working with her band, Elliott also began featuring on tracks for other rappers like Men of Vizion and Sean Diddy Combs.

Empowering Through Music

1996 gave a big milestone for Missy Elliott as she was granted her record label, The Goldmine Inc., which enabled her to work under Elektra but create, write and produce her songs. Her solo debut album, released a year later, titled Supa Dupa Fly which, became certified platinum, earning her a Grammy nomination. She released her second album in 1997, Da Real World, which became a massive success, and her follow-up album Miss E… So Addictive won two Grammy Awards.

As mentioned by PopSugar, Elliott has been a champion of breaking barriers and an inspiration to women of color in a male-dominated rap industry. She created female-empowering music and an individual style of music when male rappers overshadowed her accomplishments. With her bold outfits and narratives in music videos, Elliott began a conversation about feminism long before it was mainstream. With hit songs like Get Ur Freak On and Work It, Elliott became the best-selling female rapper in the United States. Missy Elliott released three more studio albums, Under Construction, This Is Not a Test!, and The Cookbook.

Paving The Way For Future Female Rappers

Since the release of her sixth album in 2006, Elliott has continued producing music for other artists like Monica and Jennifer Hudson. Her feature on Katy Perry’s Last Friday Night (T.G.I.F) helped the single reach the No.1 spot on Billboard Hot 100. She also performed the song as a featured guest during Katy Perry’s 2015 Super Bowl Halftime Show. In 2019, Missy Elliott was inducted into the Songwriters Hall of Fame for her contribution as a songwriter, producer, and rapper. She became the first female rapper to accomplish the feat. Queen Latifah and Michelle Obama gave heartfelt speeches during the historic ceremony.

Along with releasing the EP Iconology in 2019, Elliot featured in Dua Lipa’s remix version of Levitating with Madonna. In 2022, her six albums became RIAA platinum hits, and she became the first female rapper to achieve the feat, as stated by HypeBeast. The achievement did not go unnoticed as the rapper took to social media to voice her joy and encourage more women to keep creating music in the R&B genre.

Through breaking barriers and accomplishing historical feats, Missy Elliott has continued to pave the way for women in the music industry, especially women of color, who are sometimes unheard of due to the popularity of male artists. Through creating her music and inspiring millions, Missy Elliott continues to inspire people with her success story.
