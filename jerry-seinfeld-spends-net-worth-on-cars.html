Comedian Jerry Seinfeld has made a lot of money over the years through his titular sitcom, Seinfeld, which aired from 1989 to 1998. That’s right: Jerry Seinfeld’s Porsche collection has continued to intrigue and bewilder fans throughout the decades, and knowing it's worth more than some other celebrities' careers only makes it all the more intriguing.

Jerry Seinfeld has a net worth of $950 million. He earns $20-50 million a year from Seinfeld, which has continued to draw in big bucks over the years. In fact, it was rumored that Jerry turned down a $110 million payday to make a tenth season of Seinfeld.

Over the years, Seinfeld has put that money to good use. In addition to Jerry financing his children’s educations at the finest schools, among other splurges, he also started to create a collection of cars.

Jerry's total car collection is estimated to be valued between $50 to $150 million and stored in a $1.4 million convertible garage in Manhattan.

How Many Porsche Cars Does Jerry Seinfeld Have?

Jerry Seinfeld has a lot of Porsche cars in his collection!

There are around 150 different types of cars reportedly sheltered in Jerry Seinfeld’s lavish garage. Some of the most lavish cars in his collection are the 1957 Fiat 500, the 1958 Porsche 356A Speedster, the 1964 Aston Martin DB5 Coupe, the 1955 Porsche 550 RS, and various other vehicles.

However, his crown jewel is said to be the 1949 Porsche 356/2 Gmund, which was only one of fifty hand-built cars built in Porsche’s first factory in Austria. He also spent $650,000 to purchase and restore a Porsche 911 – the first of its kind imported to the United States. The vehicle had previously been owned by Ferry Porsche, the son of Porsche’s founder.

According to the Porsche retailer in Palm Springs, Jerry Seinfeld is said to be the most prolific Porsche collector in the world. They note that he has bought and auctioned off so many that it’s impossible to know the exact number of Porsches he has in his collection at any given time, but they believe that number to be around fifty.

Does Jerry Seinfeld own a 959 Porsche?

Even though it’s illegal to drive in the United States, Jerry Seinfeld also owns one of the only three-hundred and thirty-seven Porsche 959s ever made. According to the Palm Springs Porsche retailer, the car was released in 1986 with a flat-six turbocharged engine and a top speed of 197 miles per hour.

Although the supercharged engine made it illegal to drive on public roads, Jerry paid $700,000 for the Porsche 959. While he might not be able to take it out for a spin on Comedians In Cars Getting Coffee, it still makes a pretty valuable Porsche to keep in his collection.

Which celebrity owns one of the largest Porsche collections?

According to the Porsche website, Magnus Walker has one of the world’s largest collections of Porsche cars. Stored in an old factory building in Los Angeles, the fashion designer and automobile enthusiast bought his first Porsche – a Porsche 911 – for only $7,500.

Although Seinfeld has been known to buy and sell his vehicles, Walker does not resell them very often. In fact, he often takes cars that are “fit for the junkyard” and then spruces them up to create truly incredible one-of-a-kind vehicles that are more like works of art.

So why are celebrities so drawn to Porsche cars? As Jerry Seinfeld once said on an episode of CNBC’s Jay Leno’s Garage, “This little car is the essence of sports-car perfection.”
