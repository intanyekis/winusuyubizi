Andrew Wiggins Wiki

He was born on 23 February 1995, in Thornhill, Canada. He is a Canadian professional basketball player for the National Basketball Association's Golden State Warriors (NBA). After one season with the Kansas Jayhawks, he was taken as the first overall choice in the 2014 NBA draught by the Cleveland Cavaliers. Before being picked by the Cleveland Cavaliers, Wiggins played college basketball for the Kansas Jayhawks. He was only the second Canadian to be chosen first overall in the NBA draught. Wiggins was named NBA Rookie of the Year in 2014–15 while playing for the Minnesota Timberwolves following a preseason trade. Wiggins was moved to Golden State in February 2020, after spending his first five and a half seasons with Minnesota.

Andrew Wiggins Wiki, Early Life/Career

Early Life: - Wiggins was born in Toronto and reared in the adjacent Vaughan neighborhood of Thornhill, Ontario. He is the son of American former NBA player Mitchell Wiggins and Canadian Olympic track and field sprinter Marita Payne-Wiggins, who is originally from Barbados.

His parents met as Florida State University student-athletes. He went to Glen Shields Public School for elementary school and Vaughan Secondary School for high school, both in Vaughan. Wiggins began playing organized basketball at the age of nine when he joined a Toronto under-10 squad. He was 5 feet 7 inches tall at the time (1.70 m).

Career: - Wiggins spent his first two years of secondary school at Vaughan Secondary School in Vaughan. In his sophomore year, 2010–11, he led the school's AAAA basketball team to a 44–1 record and the Ontario provincial title.

He scored 25 points and collected 13 rebounds in the final game, despite audience yells of "Over-rated!" He transferred to Huntington Prep School in Huntington, West Virginia, in 2011. He averaged 24.2 points, 8.5 rebounds, 4.1 assists, and 2.7 blocks per game as a junior. He averaged 23.4 points, 11.2 rebounds, 2.6 blocks, and 2.5 assists per game as a senior.
