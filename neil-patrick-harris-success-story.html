Neil Patrick Harris Was Nominated For The Golden Globes At 15

He was born to lawyer parents on June 15th, 1973, in Albuquerque, New Mexico. He was an excellent student and graduated in 1991. According to The Guardian, Neil directed plays at the local theater by the age of 10. He was also interested in music and sang in the local church choir. His choir director urged him to attend a drama summer camp by legendary playwright Mark Medoff. He landed a role in Clara’s Heart (1988) beside Whoopi Goldberg at the behest of Medoff. The film performed well at the box office, collecting an estimated $5,194,491. Harris received accolades for his role and earned his first Golden Globe nomination for Best Supporting Actor. He went on to debut on television screens at fifteen with the medical drama Doogie Howser MD (1989-1993). Once again, receiving a Golden Globe nomination for Best Actor in a Television series.

After the end of the medical sitcom, he focused on films and starred in the sci-fi classic Starship Troopers (1997). The film collected an estimated $121 million at the global box office. In 1999 he starred in a mini-series titled Joan of Arc. By 2001 he turned his attention to stage productions like Sweeney Todd and Proof. The 2003 stage production Cabaret received critical acclaim and established his name as an excellent theater artist. The 2004 comedy series Harold and Kumar was his actual stepping stone to success. The parody role secured him one of the most lucrative deals in television history - How I Met Your Mother.

Neil Patrick Harris’ Most Lucrative Roles

The commitment-phobic Barney Stinson character from How I Met Your Mother earned Patrick four Emmy Award nominations since 2005. He was paid $225,000 per episode at the show's beginning, and it gradually increased with his flaming popularity. According to Insider, he collected an estimated $400,000 paycheck per episode at the show's peak. He made a total of $18 million alone from the hit sitcom in 2014. He still makes millions through royalties and re-runs of the nine-season series. The show ran from 2005-2014 and won ten Emmys. During the two decades of meteoric success, he also appeared in guest roles on hit tv shows Glee and Sesame Street.

His acting prowess accelerated with the 2014 Gone Girl, he was highly praised by the critics, and the film collected an estimated $369.3 million at the box office. In 2016 he starred in the television series A Series of Unfortunate Events in a negative role that drew wide admiration from the audience. In 2021 Harris returned to the big screen with the iconic Matrix franchise, the fourth edition - Matrix Resurrections, earning $157.3 million at the global box office.

This Is Neil Patrick Harris's Net Worth As Of 2023

The estimated net worth as of 2023 is $40 million, and Neil earns most of this from his television acting career. Forbessaid he was among the highest-paid television actors in 2014. With a $3 million American Airlines brand endorsement deal, the Uncoupled actor’s wealth grew in leaps and bounds. He forayed into voice acting with successful film franchises like Cloudy with a Chance of Meatballs parts 1 and 2 (2009 and 2013), and it earned $243 million at the box office. The Smurfs (2011) earned $563 million globally. The Smurfs 2 (2013) collected $347.5 million at the global box office.

He also essays the role of a successful emcee and has hosted numerous high-profile award functions. According to The Event Chronicle, the How I Met Your Mother actor hosted the 2013 Primetime Emmy Awards and walked away with a $1 million paycheck plus a $500,000 cash payout for one night gig, including hotel stay benefits and airfare expenses. He has also hosted the Tony awards four years in a row and the 2015 Oscars. With television and film under his credit, the Hedwig actor is also a published author. His memoir Choose Your Own Autobiography (2014) is a New York Times bestseller. He has also heavily invested in real estate properties, and in 2013, he purchased an 8,000 square feet estate in Harlem for an estimated $3.6 million. He sold this property for $7.1 million in 2022 after major renovations. In 2017 he bought a $5.5 million 13.5 acre-estate in East Hampton, New York.

With multiple accolades and successful ventures, Neil Patrick Harris is the poster child of Hollywood. He has managed to amass millions with his varied set of talents in the entertainment industry.
